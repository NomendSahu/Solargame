MERCURY

The smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. Mercury races around the Sun faster than any other planet, completing an orbit in just 88 Earth days. Despite being scorching hot on its sunlit side, the dark side can be freezing cold.

Mercury Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -290°F to 800°F
  • 📏 Diameter: 4,879 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 59 Earth days
  • 🔄 Year Length: 88 Earth days
  • 🌙 Moons: 0
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: Named after the Roman messenger god!

VENUS

The hottest planet in our solar system, even though Mercury is closer to the Sun. Venus is shrouded in thick clouds of sulfuric acid that trap heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. It rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning the Sun rises in the west!

Venus Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: 900°F average
  • 📏 Diameter: 12,104 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 243 Earth days
  • 🔄 Year Length: 225 Earth days
  • 🌙 Moons: 0
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: A day is longer than a year!

EARTH

Our home planet! Earth is the only known planet to harbor life. It has liquid water on its surface, a protective atmosphere, and a moderate climate. The perfect distance from the Sun allows for the incredible diversity of life we see today.

Earth Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -128°F to 136°F
  • 📏 Diameter: 12,742 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 24 hours
  • 🔄 Year Length: 365.25 days
  • 🌙 Moons: 1 (The Moon)
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: 71% covered in water!

MARS

The Red Planet! Mars gets its reddish color from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has the largest volcano in the solar system (Olympus Mons) and a canyon system that dwarfs the Grand Canyon. Scientists are actively searching for signs of ancient microbial life.

Mars Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -195°F to 70°F
  • 📏 Diameter: 6,779 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 24.6 hours
  • 🔄 Year Length: 687 Earth days
  • 🌙 Moons: 2 (Phobos & Deimos)
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: Home to the largest volcano!

JUPITER

The largest planet in our solar system! Jupiter is a gas giant with a famous Great Red Spot—a giant storm that has raged for hundreds of years. It has a powerful magnetic field and acts as a cosmic vacuum cleaner, protecting Earth from asteroids.

Jupiter Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -234°F average
  • 📏 Diameter: 139,820 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 10 hours
  • 🔄 Year Length: 12 Earth years
  • 🌙 Moons: 95+ known moons
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: Could fit 1,300 Earths inside!

SATURN

The magnificent ringed planet! Saturn is the second-largest planet and is famous for its vast, complex system of rings made of ice particles and rock fragments. It's a gas giant, but less dense than water—it would float in a giant bathtub!

Saturn Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -288°F average
  • 📏 Diameter: 120,536 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 10.7 hours
  • 🔄 Year Length: 29.5 Earth years
  • 🌙 Moons: 146+ known moons
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: Its rings are 90% water ice!

URANUS

The ice giant that spins on its side! Uranus is unique because its axis of rotation is tilted almost parallel to its orbit, likely due to a massive ancient collision. This results in extreme, decades-long seasons on the planet.

Uranus Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -357°F average
  • 📏 Diameter: 51,118 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 17.2 hours (retrograde)
  • 🔄 Year Length: 84 Earth years
  • 🌙 Moons: 27 known moons
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: The coldest planet in the solar system!

NEPTUNE

The distant blue giant and the windiest world in the solar system. Neptune, a large ice giant, has fierce storms, including a Great Dark Spot similar to Jupiter's, and the fastest wind speeds ever recorded—up to 1,200 mph!

Neptune Facts

  • 🌡️ Temperature: -373°F average
  • 📏 Diameter: 49,244 km
  • ⏱️ Day Length: 16.1 hours
  • 🔄 Year Length: 165 Earth years
  • 🌙 Moons: 16 known moons
  • ⚡ Fun Fact: Invisible to the naked eye!
